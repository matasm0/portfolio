GDrive Viewer

Quickly and easily access your cloud files from anywhere.
Github

Overview

GDrive Viewer is a commandline application that syncs your Google Drive cloud account onto your local disk.

The Why

A while back, I had switched over to Linux for TheOdinProject, a free online WebDev program. It turns out that there isn't (or wasn't) an official Google Drive for Desktop like there was on Windows, and I really missed the convenience of it. I decided it would be a good experience, and so ignoring other better methods such as rclone or wine, I set off to reinvent the wheel.

Skills

Google provides a lot of good documentation for their services, and it was a great opportunity to learn how to read the documentation and solve problems using it. Since nobody else seemed to want to take on the task of creating a worse version of something that already exists, it was necessary that I could figure out my own solutions given what was on the documentation.

Another great skill I developed was working with APIs. At this point, I had some experience with APIs, but the bulk of it was just making calls to my own servers. This was a good opportunity to work with a professional API, with proper error checking/message sending and all that good stuff. I learned a lot about RESTful APIs, and am far more confident in working with other APIs in the future.

This project also helped a lot with my problem solving as well as planning skills as I did not expect it to turn out so complex. Making sure that changes made to the cloud are reflected locally while at the same time making sure changes made locally are reflected to the cloud is a more difficult manuever than I originally imagined. I still have a lot to work on but I feel like it has had a positive impact on the way I write new projects.
